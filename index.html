Where Embroidery
Becomes Language

Originating in ancient Persian imperial courts, Zari is the language of goldwork. We rescue this sacred art from the pages of history, draping it over the modern shoulder.

The Materials

Threads of Gold

Drum-taut silk stretched on the Adda frame. The rhythmic scrape of metal. Our karigars use the Aari needle to sew precious spiraled wires and gold sequins, locking every segment onto the fabric by hand. Every spool holds a memory of an empire.
